2D Collisions: Completely Inelastic

When you analyzed 1-D collisions, you had to pay special attention to signs: whether the velocity (and thus, momentum) was positive or negative.  This is because velocity and momentum are vectors.  Similarly, for two-dimensional collisions, you will need to pay attention to vectors, and therefore, vector components of velocity and momentum.
